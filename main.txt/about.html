What is a Cardiac Arrest, and why should I be concerned?

Cardiac arrest in newborns is a sudden loss of heart function, often caused by complications such as respiratory failure, infections, or congenital heart defects. It is a major concern in ICUs due to the vulnerability of newborns and the need for immediate, accurate detection for better survival rates.
