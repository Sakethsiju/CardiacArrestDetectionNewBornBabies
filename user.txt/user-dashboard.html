About Cardiac Arrest

What is a Cardiac Arrest, and why should I be concerned?

Cardiac arrest in newborns is a sudden loss of heart function, often caused by complications such as respiratory failure, infections, or congenital heart defects. It is a major concern in CICUs due to the vulnerability of newborns and the need for immediate, accurate detection for better survival rates.

Detecting cardiac arrests in babies at CICU's in the early stages offers several significant advantages

Improved Survival Rates
Reduced Risk of Complications
Enhanced Decision-Making
Lower Healthcare Costs
